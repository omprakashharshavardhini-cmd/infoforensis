Question:

How is DNA used in criminal identification?

Answer:

DNA profiling compares genetic markers from crime scene samples with suspects. If the markers match, the suspect may be linked to the crime.

Question:

What is Locard's Exchange Principle?

Answer:

Every contact leaves a trace. When a criminal interacts with a crime scene they leave something behind and take something away.

Question:

What is the role of forensic toxicology?

Answer:

It detects drugs, alcohol, and poison in blood and tissue samples to determine cause of death or impairment.
